Approach & Team Structure

The CoEs partner with agencies to co-create their modernization initiatives. The CoE teams are embedded on-site, lead delivery, and require autonomy to drive results.

Objectives and Solution Competencies

The GSA Centers of Excellence (CoE) were established to accelerate IT modernization across government to:

  • Improve the public experience,
  • Increase operational efficiency, and
  • Reduce legacy IT spending across the government.

To accomplish these objectives, the CoEs centralize top government tech talent, leverage private-sector best practices, and operate with a teaming mindset to collaborate across government departments and agencies.The CoE has teams focused on five functional areas:

  • Cloud Adoption
  • Contact Center
  • Customer Experience
  • Data Analytics
  • Infrastructure Optimization

    • Delivery

    The CoEs engage in a phased approach:

    • Phase 0 - (3 Months) - Engagement scoping, Inter-Agency Agreement (IAA) development, and funding identification
    • Phase I - (6 Months) - A comprehensive department-wide discovery and planning effort to identify initial and high-impact modernization projects
    • Phase II-(1-2 years) - Implementation of the agreed upon Phase I recommendations and build sustainment for continue modernization beyond CoE Phase II

    Team Structure

    The teams are intentionally designed to have a unique blend of our customer detailees, GSA, and Industry Partners to deliver innovative solutions leveraging best practices and multiple perspectives.
