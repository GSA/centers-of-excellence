Infrastructure Optimization Playbook

Introduction

The Infrastructure Optimization Center of Excellence will assist the strategic planning activities of federal agencies through development of a unified and optimized IT infrastructure, and by leveraging federally owned data centers, cloud computing services, and commercial data centers (e.g. co-location).

Purpose

The objective of the playbook is to assist agencies in their development and implementation of infrastructure optimization, integrated cloud computing, and data center consolidation strategies. This playbook is not a one size fits all solution, or a comprehensive guide to modernization success at your agency. We hope that agencies will utilize the plays, checklists, and key questions within the playbook to enhance their modernization efforts. The playbook includes required process workflows, standard operating checklists and recommendations that should shape a consistent response in an agency’s journey of IT modernization.

Play 1

Define the Objectives, Roles, and Responsibilities for Infrastructure Optimization

To successfully optimize an agency’s infrastructure portfolio, it is important to start by prioritizing the process objectives; such as reducing costs, building IT capabilities, improving customer satisfaction, and mitigating risk. These objectives will help guide the optimization initiatives that are pursued and what message is communicated to the broader organization.

The agency should then define the roles and responsibilities of all stakeholders who will be involved in the planning and execution of the initiatives. This analysis often takes the form of a RACI (Responsible, Accountable, Consulted, Informed) diagram, which defines the exact roles and responsibilities of stakeholders against the planning activities to be supported. Without such an analysis, there is no clear delineation of responsibilities. Alignment of objectives, roles, and responsibilities will form the foundation of an optimization initiative that enables agencies to move on to subsequent plays.

Checklist

  1. Determine each objectives criteria for success
  2. Prioritize the objectives of the optimization
  3. Define roles that are distinct and exhaustive
  4. Describe activities at a granular level to clearly delineate responsibilities
  5. Although multiple individuals may be consulted or informed of decisions, ensure that there is a single party responsible for a given activity

Key Questions

  • What are the objectives of the agency and how do they overlap with the optimization initiative?
  • What qualifies as success?
  • What are the high-level activities that need to begin the optimization process?
  • Who will be involved?
  • Who will be responsible for each activity?
Play 2

Conduct a Data Center Discovery Assessment

One of of the objectives of the data center discovery assessment is to compile an IT asset inventory that includes an exhaustive list of applications hosted in the agency’s data centers that may need to be optimized and migrated. From this list, one can gather enough information to enable application rationalization and determine configurations for future hosting solutions. An IT asset inventory is also important for progress tracking and for estimating savings and workload during and after the optimization process.

A complete inventory can be developed by compiling information from pre-existing inventories, lists, and records. For example, server inventories provide lists of physical and virtual servers that can be used to determine space requirements for co-location solutions and instance requirements for service solutions. A software inventory that is likely already in possession allows for current software licenses to be categorized and provides determination of abilities to migration to a new hosting solution. Finally, a physical asset inventory will help determine the amount of racks and equipment that may need to be transported or disposed of during a migration.

Checklist

  1. Compile a complete inventory of all applications, servers, software licenses, data sources and their dependencies
  2. Provide a tool to enable automated collection of network data about application/server communication
  3. Build a cost baseline for each data center that includes all of its asset categories (e.g., rent, power, operations and maintenance (O&M), server labor, server hardware, server software, storage, data center labor, hosting infrastructure and hosting services.)
  4. Validate the collected data with data center managers and other key stakeholders

Key Questions

  • How can the data collection process be standardized to ensure consistency and quality across the organization?
  • How can an agency ensure that it has an exhaustive list of its physical and virtual assets?
  • How does the portfolio optimization “bottoms-up” figure compare with the “top-down” cost summary?
  • If the costs of optimizing your portfolio are drastically different, how will the differences be reconciled?
  • Are there strategies that can be used in the future to automate the collection and maintenance of inventories?
Play 3

Conduct Application Rationalization

The process of application rationalization involves the objective disposition of applications in an agency’s portfolio based on an evaluation of an application’s business value and technical vitality. Application rationalization seeks to first eliminate applications that are no longer needed or that provide low value relative to their cost. Applications that are retained can be dispositioned based on their capability and fit for a given environment (e.g., dedicated hosting, commercial cloud). Disposition of the portfolio provides the agency with a plan for its existing IT inventory, savings and cost avoidance opportunities, and identifies applications to be invested in and potentially candidates for migration to the cloud.

Checklist

  1. Align on the objective of the application rationalization (e.g., standardization, cost reduction, modernization)
  2. Involve the business and application owners in the rationalization process
  3. Rationalize applications through an objective and structured framework, which has a repeatable process and allows the agency to conduct application disposition
  4. Determine potential solutions for applications with high hosting costs (e.g., a simple lift-and-shift to a cloud environment)
  5. Identify alternative hosting solutions if a cloud migration is not possible (e.g., local data center)
  6. Determine which applications could be evaluated for cloud readiness planning and assessment
  7. Run technical assessments to identify landing zone (Infrastructure as a Service [IaaS], Platform as a Service [PaaS], Software as a Service [SaaS])
  8. Explore commercial-off-the-shelf (COTS) or Government-off-the-shelf (GOTS) software solutions that could provide similar functionality

Key Questions

  • Have both the business and IT organizations had a chance to weigh in on the application rationalization?
  • How can the process be standardized to ensure consistent criteria is applied to evaluate applications?
  • Has the agency attempted to map applications against a cross-agency functional map to identify overlaps?
  • Of the applications to eliminate, what levers can be applied to capture value, and where are they are to be hosted?
  • Is there an opportunity to leverage network and compute statistics to visualize application dependencies and enable fact-based discussions?
Play 4

Consolidate Data Centers

By reducing the number of data centers via consolidations, organizations can reduce costs due to a smaller network and application infrastructure. The consolidation increases the control as it promotes optimization through transport, architecture and options to deploy more advanced protocols, and management strategies that maximize bandwidth utilization and performance of the network. The consolidation also reduces the number of physical sites and assets to manage. The lower number of sites means they are easier to physically secure and enterprise initiatives become far easier, less costly, and requires fewer resources. The disaster recovery solutions are also less daunting due to all the vital components residing in in one place, easing replication and failover initiation. Data center consolidation supports compliance efforts by promoting process and system automation and standardization. The automation captures the procedures and functions that are executed, and ensures compliance with relevant policies, regulations, standards, and quality of service metrics.

Checklist

  1. Align on the objectives for the consolidation (e.g., cost reduction, process standardization, increased security)
  2. Baseline the existing data centers and take an inventory of their physical and virtual assets (see play 2)
  3. Identify the right number of end-state centers and disaster recovery solutions
  4. Identify opportunities for shared services and resources
  5. Request data center consolidation plans from each facility to be closed
  6. Develop a framework to assess migration plans and provided feedback to improve execution
  7. Test applications to ensure continuity of service
  8. Install and configure the applications while accounting for changes to the network or environment
  9. Close facility and reclaim expenditures (e.g., rent, power)

Key Questions

  • Has there been an identification of migration scenarios and their potential tradeoffs and any necessary requirements?
  • Will the evaluation of each migration scenario include with a cost benefit analysis and risk assessment?
  • What will be the timeline for the consolidation?
  • Are there additional, ongoing initiatives that may synergize or complicate the consolidation?
  • How will migration plans be evaluated?
  • What will serve as a forcing function to ensure closure?
  • How will cost savings be captured?
Play 5

Obtain Authority to Test/Operate

The Authority to Operate (ATO) represents the formal management approval to place a system into operation. An ATO shall be granted after the IT system fully complies with the Certification and Accreditation (C&A) process. The process must be compliant with the regulations specified in the C&A process. The C&A process contains the modules of Security Certification, Security Accreditation, E-Authentication and Business Continuity Planning.

An ATO is granted after the system has been certified that it has passed all requirements to become operational. As a perquisite to ATO, organizations must follow documented procedures for first securing an Authority to Test (ATT).

Checklist

  1. Contact organization security team to ensure they are informed of your intentions
  2. Determine the requirements and compile the appropriate documentation for an ATT
  3. Ensure the systems to be used for the ATT are updated and compliant with security policy
  4. Work within the organization’s security apparatus to meet other policy requirements
  5. Prepare your team to begin testing immediately in order to maximize the use of the ATT duration
  6. Obtain approval from the Chief Information Security Officer
  7. Acknowledge common barriers to proactively work towards overcoming such areas as 2-factor authentication, risk remediation, boundary protections, safeguarding of unauthorized shared usages as well as cryptographic safeguards to preserve confidentiality and data integrity.

Key Questions

  • Which services do you currently operate that will require an ATT or ATO?
  • Does your organization’s ATO process contain a repeatable process with an acceptable time-frame?
  • Does the ATO process allow for inheritable controls from an enterprise level?
Play 6

Achieve Operational Excellence

Achieving operational excellence means improving the performance or cost-efficiency of their IT operations. In the context of IT infrastructure, this often requires an organization to become more efficient in managing its resources, processes, and procurement. Agencies should determine whether the staff has the skills necessary to implement its strategic vision and create strategies to address any talent gaps. Furthermore, organization should look to centralize or pool similar roles at the appropriate organization level to match labor supply and incoming demand. From a process perspective, agencies should try to eliminate burdensome or low-value steps in their core IT processes (e.g., manual patching or server provisioning). Finally, the agency should practice demand management by only purchasing what is necessary (e.g., not overspecing on servers). Agencies should also optimize its sourcing process, acknowledge areas to optimize, rationalize, or reduce their financial spend based on target benchmarks.

Checklist

  1. Conduct user-backed operational assessment to identify customer pain points and improvement opportunities
  2. Right size 'business’ and ‘technical operations’ support functions to remove low and non-value add roles
  3. Streamline processes to cut out unnecessary steps, thereby reducing labor requirements.
  4. Increase system usage, efficiency and virtualization
  5. Adopt a mindset of waste reduction / continuous improvement
  6. Optimize hardware and software procurement
  7. Rigorously track operational efficiency through Key Performance Indicators. (KPI’s)
  8. Redesign, reimage, and standardize processes to reduce resourcing levels
  9. Identify necessary skills and assess current staff to create a strategy for training

Key Questions

  • How will you measure improvements to customer satisfaction and in quality and consistency in response to customers?
  • Has centralization of similar roles at the appropriate organization level been made to match labor supply and incoming demand been identified?
  • Are guidelines to procure the “right” quantities, at the “right” organization level been established to purchase more efficiently?
  • What are the main processes involved in operating the IT infrastructure and where are the manual steps that could be automated or streamlined?
Play 7

Create an Agile Infrastructure

There are major shifts needed to become an agile infrastructure. Cross-functional product teams must own and specifically define items from the service catalog the infrastructure provides. These teams must fulfill requests for their services and improve service delivery by streamlining processes and engineering automated solutions. The engineering of the talent cultivation will provide an organization of well-rounded engineers who can focus on constantly learning new skills and the development of infrastructure products. Redesigning products using an agile approach eliminates unnecessary steps and improves the process, making the process highly effective and streamlined. All typical lean levers are utilized to ensure minimal to no waste in the system. The creation of end-to-end automation requires that the IT infrastructure focus on the development of standard consumable infrastructure products. Self-service / Application programming interface (API) driven and automated provisioning of standardized products should ensure scalability of configurations.

Checklist

  1. Pilot agile through a cross-functional team of analysts, user experience designers, testers, developers, and product owners
  2. Adopt agile processes through structured backlog, daily scrums, weekly sprints, and regular retrospectives
  3. Leverage existing automation to accelerate service delivery
  4. Scale pilot model to launch new transformations

Key Questions

  • Is there a cultural appetite to undergo this type of transformation?
  • Where is the starting point? (e.g., a specific agency or with a pilot team)
  • Are the organizations willing to engage on this type of transformation, particularly by bringing in the product teams?
  • Are the organizational team leads willing to set priorities and controls for budget based on targeted KPIs related to an agile infrastructure?
  • Are the product owner's driving product vision and strategy for the teams?
  • Do team leads ensure consistency and governance across for cross-cutting areas?
Play 8

Implement Automation

Faced with increased demands for faster service, IT modernization struggles to scale and meet business needs. A varying number of tools and processes add further complexity, intensifying compliance and ongoing security risks. These factors all lead to unwelcome consequences; high-stakes vulnerability and security exposure, inefficient IT, and slow infrastructure service delivery. The implementation of automations allows the organization to enable continuous delivery of proficient services. Achieving automation produces the end-result, which often decreases costs, increased speed and efficiency and improved security.

Checklist

  1. Align on the objectives and processes to undergo automation
  2. Determine how to measure progress (e.g., which KPIs or metrics to use)
  3. Conduct diagnostic of infrastructure processes and tasks to focus the automation efforts (e.g., incident response, provisioning)
  4. Identify the best solution (e.g., robotic process automation [RPA], interactive voice response [IVR]) and build appropriate toolsets and processes
  5. Establish governance structure to ensure value capture
  6. Determine how to best leverage spare capacity from existing resources
  7. Drive change management by communicating to stakeholders

Key Questions

  • What are the core processes in your IT operation?
  • How do you currently measure performance for each of your core processes?
  • Where are the pain points in your current processes?
  • Where can technology be leveraged to free up resources for more value-add activities?
  • Are your digital leaders taking an end-to-end approach to automation across the entire IT value chain?
  • Has the organization become agile and enabled continuous delivery from code check-in to production in a standard minimal timeframe?
Play 9

Develop a Communication Plan

A well-defined communication plan is a key component of change management and ensuring the mission of optimization is understood by all staff members. The communication strategy should be tailored to the agency. Leadership needs to decide on what needs to be communicated, who to communicate it to, when to communicate, and the appropriate format of communication. The goal is for the staff to feel connected to the optimization and that they are bought into the strategy. Agencies should also provide a forum to gather feedback and initiate discussion.

Checklist

  1. Align on communication objectives and key stories to share
  2. Determine the sequence of communication and the right stakeholders to share the communications with
  3. Identify the best format of communication
  4. Draft the communication strategy and output so it is documented
  5. Provide important updates onto the intranet
  6. Determine if there is a need for a larger town hall or all-hands meeting

Key Questions

  • What are the main points that need to be communicated to all staff members to support initiatives?
  • When should communications be issued for each level of the organization?
  • What medium of communications does the organization best respond to?
  • What is the best way to respond to additional questions or inquiries?
Play 10

Ensure Ongoing Executive Reporting and Interaction

Regular reporting on the current progress of the optimization efforts will ensure that the leadership and the organization are aligned. Reporting will also enable to agencies to identify any issues or risks and rapidly escalate them to the appropriate source for resolution. Agencies will need to align on the most relevant metrics and build a dashboard for tracking. Most importantly, the information needs to be structured in a way that will allow decisions to be made and actions to be taken; reporting should not be done for reporting’s sake.

Checklist

  1. Identify the main objectives that will be tracked
  2. Determine the best metrics to measure progress
  3. Identify a consistent source of data that could be used for the reporting
  4. Determine a cadence for the reporting with key stakeholders
  5. Build a path for escalating key risks or issues
  6. Leverage the reporting as a method to build additional accountability into an organization

Key Questions

  • What are the key objectives that are being driven and what are the best ways to measure progress?
  • Who should be responsible for the reporting and data management?
  • Where will the data for reporting come from?
  • Who should see the report out and how frequently should the reports be shared?
  • Is there a path for escalating key risks or issues?
  • What type of actions can be taken if the report shows progress is behind schedule?
